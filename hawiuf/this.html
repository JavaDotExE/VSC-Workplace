Accountancy, Business
and Management

Accountancy, Business and Management strand marries creativity, mathematical application, and business sense to prepare the best business professionals of tomorrow.

The ABM strand curriculum was constructed to shape senior high school students that want to forge their own paths in the areas of accountancy, business, and entrepreneurship.

Pros of Choosing ABM Strand:

Business and Financial Literacy: ABM provides a strong foundation in business and financial concepts, which can be valuable for future career opportunities and personal finance management.

Preparation for Business Courses: If you plan to pursue a degree in business, accounting, finance, or related fields, ABM can give you a head start by introducing you to key concepts and skills.

Entrepreneurial Skills: ABM often includes entrepreneurship subjects, helping students develop the skills and mindset needed to start and manage their own businesses.

Wide Range of Career Options: ABM opens doors to various career paths, including business management, finance, marketing, human resources, and more.

Problem-Solving Skills: It fosters critical thinking and problem-solving abilities, which are valuable in various professions and daily life.

Cons of Choosing ABM Strand:

Narrower Focus: ABM is more specialized in business-related subjects, which means you might have limited exposure to other fields of study, such as science or technology.

Mathematics Intensity: ABM typically includes advanced mathematics courses like calculus and statistics, which can be challenging for some students.

Competition: Many students choose ABM due to its popularity, leading to potentially high competition for college slots and job opportunities in related fields.

Rigorous Curriculum: The workload in ABM can be demanding, requiring strong study habits and time management skills.
